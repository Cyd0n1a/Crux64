Int. Camp two, perched on a rocky outcropping. Morning of day two. The meditation minigame has concluded.

MOUNT XERXES. CAMP TWO.

Scene 03: The Circling Hour

[SCENE START]

VISUAL:
Fade in from black, slow, like an eyelid easing open. The vignette from the night before is gone; the screen feels lighter, cleaner. Pale blue morning light washes over the rock ledge. Condensation beads on the outside of the sleeping bag.

AUDIO:
Total stillness at first. Then, gradually: the tick of cooling propane metal, a single bird call somewhere far below, the low moan of wind moving through a distant col.

UI PROMPT:
[ Push Forward to sit up ]

The player sits up. The camera lingers a beat longer than usual on the horizon line, where the sun hasn't yet cleared the ridge.

UI PROMPT:
[ Press (Interact) to check gear ]

The player runs a hand along the frost-stiffened straps of the pack, checking buckles out of habit more than necessity.

AUDIO:
The gentle, familiar jingle. The Bluetooth earpiece connects with its soft static bloom.

MAYA (V.O.)
(Voice a little hoarse, like she just woke up too, even though it's her evening)
Morning, mountain goat. Vitals look steady. How'd the sit go?

PLAYER
(Stretching, audible yawn)
Quiet. Quieter than I expected. My head usually won't shut up, but out here it just... did.

MAYA (V.O.)
Good. That's good, Kit.
(Beat)
Sorry - that's a new one. Camp-two-you sounds different than base-camp-you. Softer.

PLAYER
Softer, huh. Don't get used to it. Altitude's just knocked the sarcasm out of me temporarily.

MAYA (V.O.)
(Laughs, warmer than last night)
There it is. Okay. Talk me through the view. I need the postcard version, I'm stuck in a meeting in forty minutes.

PLAYER
(Turning slowly, taking in the vista)
Sky's gone that pale, watery blue. Like it hasn't decided on a color yet. Rock's rust-red where the sun's starting to touch it. And Xerxes just... keeps going up. I thought yesterday's view was the whole mountain. It wasn't even a third of it.

MAYA (V.O.)
(Softly)
He'd have had something poetic to say about that. You know - the "the summit humbles you twice" thing he always said. Once from the bottom, once from the-

PLAYER
(Cutting in, gently)
Once from the top. Yeah. I remember.
(A pause, weighted but not heavy)
I keep waiting to feel him here. Haven't yet. Maybe higher up.

MAYA (V.O.)
Maybe. Or maybe he's just letting you have the quiet first.

(A shadow sweeps briefly across the rock ledge, gone as fast as it appeared. The player looks up.)

VISUAL:
High above, riding a thermal along the cliff face, a golden eagle banks into view. Its wings are still, angled, catching the rising air. It is enormous even at this distance - a dark, deliberate shape against the pale sky.

UI PROMPT:
[ Look Up to track the eagle ]

AUDIO:
The wind briefly drops away, as if the whole valley is holding its breath. A faint, high, keening cry drifts down from the bird.

PLAYER
(Quiet, almost reverent)
Maya. There's an eagle. Huge - must be a golden. It's just... hanging there, barely moving.

MAYA (V.O.)
(A smile in her voice)
Send a picture if you can. Actually - no, don't, save the battery. Just tell me about it.

(The eagle tilts, one wingtip dipping. Its circling tightens, spiraling lower and lower down the cliff face in slow, unhurried loops.)

PLAYER
It's circling something. Getting lower each pass. There - on that scree slope, maybe two hundred meters down. A marmot, I think, out sunning itself. Doesn't even know.

MAYA (V.O.)
(A beat, then, careful)
You don't have to narrate this part if you don't want to.

PLAYER
No - no, I want to watch. It feels wrong to look away.

(The eagle's circling stops. For a long moment it simply hangs on the thermal, motionless, calculating. Then, without any visible transition, it folds its wings and drops.)

VISUAL:
The stoop is impossibly fast - a controlled, plummeting line, wings tucked to a blade. The camera can barely track it. At the last instant the wings snap open, talons forward, and the shape disappears behind a fold of rock. A single, sharp cry. Then silence.

PLAYER
(A long exhale)
...Got it, I think. Didn't see the end of it. Rock was in the way.

MAYA (V.O.)
(Gently)
That's the mountain too, you know. Not just the pretty light on the ridge. The whole of it.

PLAYER
(Thoughtful)
Yeah. I don't know why, but that actually helps somehow. Everything up here just... does what it's built to do. No hesitation.

MAYA (V.O.)
Maybe borrow a little of that today. Less hesitation, more moving forward.

PLAYER
(A small, real laugh)
Noted, coach.

MAYA (V.O.)
I've got to run - this meeting won't chair itself, unfortunately. Second ridge is your next checkpoint. Eat something before you climb, please. Actual food, not just his tea.

PLAYER
Yes, ma'am. Talk soon, Maya.

MAYA (V.O.)
Talk soon. Go get humbled by a mountain, mountain goat.

AUDIO:
Beep. The line disconnects. The wind returns to the valley, filling back in around the silence, and somewhere far below, a single feather drifts down onto the scree.
